  7. A false positive occurs when you conclude a significant difference, even though there is no real difference in the underlying population from which the sample came. Example 1: If we conclude a medicine works significantly better than a placebo in the sample, but it does not actually work on average in the population, then we have a false positive for the effect of the medicine. Example 2: Say we estimate the ratio of black to white sheep in a large farm by drawing a random sample. A false postive would occur if in the sample, the ratio differed significantly from \(1 : 1\), even though it does not in the whole farm. False positives are also called type I errors.↩︎
